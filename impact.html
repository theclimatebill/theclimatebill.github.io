Chapter, who pays the price
The measured impact

What warming is doing to life on Earth.

Heatwaves that last longer than they used to. Drought that turns green land to dust. Reefs losing their colour as the water around them warms. The sections below put numbers to each of these.

Heat and drought124 million more people faced food insecurity in 2023 due to droughts and heatwaves.
Key findings
  • Heat now kills an estimated 546,000 people a year, a figure up 23% since the 1990s.
  • Wildlife populations have fallen by 73% on average since 1970, with Latin America's decline the steepest of any region.
  • Climate related disasters could displace 44 to 216 million people within their own countries by 2050, depending on how much emissions are cut.

Who is actually paying

Pledged to the Loss and Damage Fund
Total pledges since COP27. Around $448 million has been disbursed. The United States withdrew its pledge in 2025.

$395bn
Estimated annual loss and damage
A 2024 study in Nature put annual climate loss and damage at around $395 billion. Total pledges to date amount to under a quarter of one percent of a single year's damage.
